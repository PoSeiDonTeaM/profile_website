Aristotle Space & Aeronautics Team (ASAT)

The Aristotle Space & Aeronautics Team seeks to promote and advance aerospace engineering throughout the university. Furthermore, the team aims to stimulate interest in aerospace, technology and science through outreach activities.

CubeSAT Project

The CubeSat team is getting started with the design of a CubeSat; a tiny satellite that can be launched as secondary payload on commercial launches, at a fraction of the cost of a traditional satellite. Its mission is to collect data of active gamma ray sources of our universe, such as quasars, black holes or AGNs, using a prototype detector constructed in the nuclear physics department of AUTh.

My Role

Working on a CubeSAT project demands a huge amount of effort from each and every member. If a subsystem does not work as expected, that affects the whole project by holding back its progress. From the second year being a member at the team, I aquired the position of On-Board-Computer Subsystem Coordinator. The job of a coordinator is to ensure that everything will go as planned and avoid any upcoming setbacks. Beyond this, working as a team sometimes may be difficult, due to some of the members' differences. So in order to achieve a good cooperation among the members, the coordinator needs to eliminate those differences.
